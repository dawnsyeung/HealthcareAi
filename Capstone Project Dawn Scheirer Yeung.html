An AI-Powered Transparency Tool for Healthcare Claims Denials

INTRODUCTION

With a relentless commitment to equity and efficiency in healthcare, EquiClaim is a bridge...a bridge that empowers independent providers with AI-driven insights into payer denial patterns. Through real-time data aggregation and predictive analytics, EquiClaim equips small to mid-sized practices with actionable transparency, leveling the information asymmetry that has long favored payers over providers and patients.

At EquiClaim, we see reimbursement fairness not as a goal, but as an ongoing journey toward a more just healthcare system.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

  • An Escalating Crisis in Claims Denials
    • 41% of healthcare providers report denial rates of 10% or higher, leading to significant rework and lost revenue (Experian Health, 2025).
    • Initial claim denials reached 11.8% in 2024, a 16% rise since 2018, with up to 15% of all medical claims denied or delayed (MGMA, 2024; Kodiak Solutions, 2025).
    • Demographically disproportionate impacts: Denials often stem from coding errors (40% of preventive care denials) and affect smaller practices hardest, exacerbating financial strain.
  • Provider-Payer Asymmetry
    • Payers leverage vast data to deny claims for profit, while providers rely on fragmented, individual experiences.
    • 70% of denied claims are eventually paid after appeals, but the process wastes resources and delays care (AHA, 2025).
  • What Does "Fair" Reimbursement Look Like?
    • No standardized visibility into denial trends by payer, geography, or diagnosis.
    • Lack of real-time insights hinders proactive decision-making, harming patient access to care.

OUR VISION

  • Transparency in a Tap
    With AI-assistance, providers gain instant access to aggregated denial patterns, empowering them to anticipate payer behaviors and document claims with precision—transforming piecemeal guesswork into data-driven strategy.
  • Predict and Prevent in Real Time
    Too often, denials are discovered after submission, wasting time and resources. EquiClaim "flags" high-risk claims proactively, suggesting adjustments to documentation or procedures before submission, ensuring faster approvals.
  • Insights Worth a Thousand Denials
    Leveraging visual dashboards, EquiClaim maps denial trends by payer, geography, and code, providing a clear, color-coded path to reimbursement success. Providers see biases and patterns at a glance, enabling informed advocacy.

MODEL STRUCTURE

  • Natural Language Processing
    In analyzing payer clinical policy bulletins and denial reason codes, we impose structure on unstructured data (e.g., medical records, remittances) to generate labels for compliance and reimbursement criteria.
  • Prediction Modeling/Visualization
    Using supervised learning on labeled historical data (approvals/denials) and unsupervised learning to cluster unknown denial patterns, we predict claim outcomes based on attributes like geography, specialty, plan type (self-funded vs. fully insured), CPT/ICD-10 codes.
  • Recommendation System
    Based on NLP and prediction models, EquiClaim provides advisory recommendations—e.g., "Add documentation for medical necessity per payer policy"—visualized in dashboards with color bands indicating denial risk (green: low, red: high).

DATA STRATEGY

  • NLP to Structure Policies and Records
    A scrub of payer policies, 835 remittances, CPT/ICD-10 codes, and anonymized patient records to identify trends in denials and compliance.
  • Critical Attributes for Contextual Insights
    Provider details (geography, specialty)
    Plan attributes (self-funded, fully insured, state affiliation)
    Social determinants (e.g., patient demographics to detect biases)
  • Consortium of Aggregated Data
    Data-Related Issues
    Quantity of data: Abundance of historical claims; real-time feeds from consenting providers for ongoing updates.
    Potential biases: Training multiple models to exclude attributes and measure impact; address overrepresentation in certain geographies or specialties.
    Privacy/HIPAA: Patient data anonymized; consent required for aggregation—providers contribute only with explicit patient opt-in.
    Label Challenges: AI labeling of policies based on standardized parameters; challenges in structuring ambiguous medical notes.
    Overfitting: Cross-validation across payers to ensure generalization.
    Generalization: Focus on commercial payers; potential expansion to Medicare/Medicaid.

IMPLEMENTATION STRATEGY

  • Focus Group
    Comprised of out-of-network providers, patients, revenue experts, and payer representatives, a focus group provides feedback on usability and ethical integration.
  • Pilot Group
    Start with a small cohort of 20-30 independent providers (low-risk, out-of-network) to test EquiClaim on anonymized data for 6-12 months, gathering user experience feedback aligned with billing cycles.
  • Seamless Integration, Not Disruption
    EquiClaim is a "bolt-on" tool that integrates with existing EHR and billing systems (e.g., via APIs), minimizing workflow changes. Providers upload claims data securely, receive insights without altering core processes—focusing on empowerment over overhaul.
